Q: How many hearts does an octopus have?
A: An octopus has three hearts. Two branchial hearts pump blood through the gills, while a third systemic heart pumps oxygenated blood to the rest of the body. Interestingly, when an octopus swims, the systemic heart stops beating, which is why octopuses prefer crawling over swimming.

Q: Can a snail sleep for years?
A: Yes! Snails can hibernate or estivate (summer sleep) for up to three years if conditions are unfavorable. They seal their shell opening with a layer of dried mucus called an epiphragm to retain moisture and wait for better weather.

Q: What animal never sleeps?
A: Bullfrogs are often cited as animals that never sleep — they show no reduced response to stimuli even during rest periods. However, most animals do sleep in some form. Dolphins and whales sleep with only half their brain at a time, a state called unihemispheric slow-wave sleep.

Q: How do cats always land on their feet?
A: Cats have a natural reflex called the "righting reflex." They can rotate their flexible spine and use their tail as a counterbalance to orient their body feet-down during a fall — all within milliseconds, even from short distances.

Q: What is the loudest animal on Earth?
A: The sperm whale holds the record, producing clicks up to 230 decibels — louder than a jet engine. Among land animals, the howler monkey can be heard up to 3 miles away, making it the loudest land animal.

Q: Do fish feel pain?
A: Research increasingly suggests yes. Fish have nociceptors (pain receptors) and show behavioral and physiological responses to harmful stimuli similar to those seen in mammals. However, whether they experience pain consciously the way humans do is still debated among scientists.

Q: Why do flamingos stand on one leg?
A: Scientists believe flamingos stand on one leg to conserve body heat. By tucking one leg against their body, they reduce the surface area exposed to cold water. Studies also show it may require less muscular effort than standing on two legs.

Q: What animal has the longest lifespan?
A: The ocean quahog clam (Arctica islandica) holds the record. One specimen named "Ming" was found to be 507 years old. Among vertebrates, the Greenland shark can live over 400 years, making it the longest-lived vertebrate known.

Q: Can elephants really remember everything?
A: Elephants have exceptional long-term memory — they can recognize individual humans and elephants they haven't seen for decades. They remember locations of water sources visited years ago and mourn their dead. The phrase "elephants never forget" has a real biological basis.

Q: How do bees communicate?
A: Honeybees communicate primarily through the "waggle dance." A forager bee dances in a figure-eight pattern on the honeycomb — the angle of the dance indicates the direction of a food source relative to the sun, and the duration indicates the distance.

Q: What is the fastest animal on Earth?
A: The peregrine falcon is the fastest animal, reaching speeds over 240 mph (386 km/h) during a hunting dive (stoop). Among horizontal-running animals, the cheetah holds the record at up to 75 mph (120 km/h).

Q: Why do zebras have stripes?
A: The leading scientific theory is that stripes deter biting insects like tsetse flies and horseflies. Studies show flies have difficulty landing on striped surfaces. Stripes may also help with heat regulation and social recognition within the herd.

Q: Can dogs see color?
A: Yes, but not as many colors as humans. Dogs are dichromats — they have two types of color receptors (humans have three). They see the world in shades of blue and yellow, and cannot distinguish red from green. Their color vision is similar to a human with red-green color blindness.

Q: How do migratory birds navigate?
A: Birds use multiple navigation systems simultaneously: the position of the sun, star patterns at night, Earth's magnetic field (detected by magnetite crystals in their beaks), landmarks, and even smell. This redundancy makes their navigation remarkably reliable.

Q: What is the only mammal capable of true flight?
A: Bats are the only mammals capable of sustained, powered flight. Flying squirrels and colugos glide but cannot generate lift. There are over 1,400 species of bats, making up about 20% of all mammal species on Earth.
