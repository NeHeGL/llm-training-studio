ℹ️ What is Training?
LoRA fine-tuning is the industry standard for customizing LLMs. It produces a full, standalone model with your knowledge and personality baked in.
💾
Step 1 — Save & Build Dataset
Add Q&A pairs in the Training Data tab, then click this to convert everything (manual entries + knowledge files) into ChatML JSONL files for training.
▶
Step 2 — Train Model (LoRA)
Fine-tunes the base model on your GPU. Expect ~10–30 min for a 7B model at 16 GB VRAM.
📦
Step 3 — Export → GGUF
Merges LoRA into the base model and saves a single .gguf file — loads in Ollama, LM Studio, and the built-in chat tester.
💬
Step 4 — Test LLM
Launches a local chat server so you can talk to your trained model immediately.
⚡
Ready to train
Add training examples in the Training Data tab, then use the Pipeline tab to train and export your model.

⭐ Recommended Settings
📖 What These Mean
4-bit QLoRA — Model weights are compressed to 4 bits. Uses ~0.5 GB per billion params. Works on most GPUs with 6+ GB VRAM. Slightly slower than full precision but produces excellent results.
Full BF16/FP16 — Model loads at full precision (~2 GB/B params). Faster training, but needs 24+ GB VRAM. Only recommended if your GPU can handle it.
Batch size — How many examples are processed together. Larger = faster but uses more VRAM. If you get OOM errors, reduce to 1.
Max seq length — Longest Q&A pair (in tokens) the model trains on. 2048 is a good default. Reduce to 1024 if you get OOM errors.
Grad checkpointing — Trades speed for memory by recomputing activations instead of storing them. Required for most GPUs under 24 GB.
